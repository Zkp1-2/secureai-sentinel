## SOP paragraph

One of my strongest independent projects is SecureAI Sentinel, an AI-assisted vulnerability triage and remediation platform. I designed it to explore how AI can support cybersecurity analysts by parsing scanner outputs, retrieving relevant security knowledge, classifying vulnerabilities, scoring risk, and generating remediation reports. The project combines FastAPI, React, local RAG, deterministic multi-agent analysis, CVSS-style risk scoring, SQLite workspaces, and scanner parsers for tools such as OWASP ZAP, Trivy, Semgrep, Nmap, and CSV-based vulnerability trackers. This project strengthened my interest in AI Security and motivated me to pursue graduate study in artificial intelligence with a long-term goal of researching secure and trustworthy AI systems.

## PhD-oriented explanation

SecureAI Sentinel represents my current research direction at the intersection of artificial intelligence and cybersecurity. Rather than building a general chatbot, I focused on a structured AI-assisted security workflow: scanner report ingestion, evidence extraction, retrieval-augmented cybersecurity context, multi-agent reasoning, risk scoring, and analyst validation. The project helped me understand the importance of grounding AI outputs in evidence, controlling hallucination risk, and designing systems where human analysts remain responsible for final decisions. I hope to extend this work toward robust AI security analysis, secure AI agents, and trustworthy automated vulnerability management.
